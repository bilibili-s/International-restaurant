  1. AMALA AND EWEDU

    Amala and ewedu is a popular Nigerian dish, especially among the Yoruba people. Amala is a starchy, dark-brown paste made from yam or cassava flour. Ewedu is a slimy, green soup made from jute leaves, often described as having a similar texture to okra. Together, they form a delicious and satisfying meal, often served with a flavorful stew and assorted meats.This is very common in the western part of Nigeria

  3. ABACHA (OR AFRICAN SALAD)
    Abacha, also known as African Salad, is a popular dish from the Igbo people of southeastern Nigeria, particularly in Anambra where it is a staple. It is made from dried, shredded cassava that is rehydrated and mixed with various ingredients like palm oil, ugba (oil bean), garden eggs, utazi leaves, and spices. This flavorful and nutritious dish is often enjoyed as a snack, side dish, or even a main meal, and is frequently associated with celebrations and social gatherings within the Igbo community. .
